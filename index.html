Above is what I studied in college, very cool isn't it? Superconductivity is known as a macroscopic quantum effect, i.e. a normal size object behaving according to the laws of quantum mechanics. But no, it's not possible to fly like superman using magnetism to levitate above ground. Due to the dipole nature of magnetism, magnetic force is relatively short-ranged. I received my Bachelor's degree in Physics from UCSD, specializing in Computational and Condensed Matter Physics. Although ceramic superconductors had brought forth a revolution, the physics behind this new class of high Tc superconductors remain a mystery.

Although Physics has always been a subject of fascination and childhood aspiration to me, I discovered that I actually enjoyed hacking/building more as a career option. What drawn me to Physics is the understanding of our natural world and the empowerment it implies. Understanding empowers us, because it implies that we possess predictive power. Thus, I have a strong inclination toward creating predictive model. I look forward to create intelligent applications using mathematics and statistical model.

Put the nerdy topics aside, I do have outdoor hobbies. I really enjoyed surfing in La Jolla. UCSD is located right next to the beach. It was a 10 minutes drive from my apartment to the beach.
